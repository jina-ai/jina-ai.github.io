Making Visuals Vocal: SceneXplain's Impact on Product Image Accessibility

SceneXplain transforms product images into audio descriptions, ensuring visual content isn't just seen, but also heard and understood. It's a step forward in creating an inclusive digital world for everyone

Alex C-G
June 29, 2023 • 9 minutes read

In a world that is increasingly connected and digital, we're experiencing an unprecedented volume of visual content. Yet, for a significant portion of the population, these visuals remain inaccessible. Visual accessibility, often referred to as 'A11y', is a cornerstone of inclusive digital design. It ensures that information is not only available but also perceivable and understandable to all, including people with visual impairments.

SceneXplain, Jina AI's image analysis tool, is here to transform online accessibility. Now you can leverage the power of artificial intelligence to generate audio descriptions for product images. With SceneXplain, images aren't just seen; they're heard and understood, ensuring that the growing volume of visual content is accessible to everyone.

Why is this so critical? For online businesses, making product images accessible can broaden their customer base, increase customer satisfaction, and improve overall brand reputation. It isn't just about inclusivity; it's also a strategic business decision.

That’s not to mention the legal side of things! We’ve written about this extensively already: Check the following post:

Bringing images to life with textual descriptions

In essence, SceneXplain's voiceover descriptions for product images are a significant step towards digital inclusivity.

Let's explore how this powerful tool works and the far-reaching impact it could have on our digital world. In this post, we'll look at how SceneXplain can transform the world of fashion e-commerce.

🖼️
We'll be using freely available images from Pexels.com in this article.

Since the day of its launch, SceneXplain has been able to use its large-language-model powers to give you a description of what's in an image:

For this blog post we're going to use the "Comet" algorithm. In our experience, it is less "creative" than the default Dune algorithm. That's what we want, considering we need accurate information rather than deep scene descriptions.

That's a nice description of the image - but if we're talking fashion, we want descriptions...of the fashion. We can do that with visual question answering by asking:

Describe each of the items of clothing in this image, in high detail,
in the form of a numbered list. Include information about color, cut,
style, fit. Do not mention the human in the picture.

Listen up: Converting images into audio descriptions

If you're an e-commerce store, you likely have some details about the specific item you're trying to showcase in your image. Often you just want to sell one part of the fashion ensemble, not the whole outfit. So assuming you've specified your item as (for example) puffy green jacket, you could ask for that in more detail:

Mastering the magic: Navigating the API

🤖
We’re updating and improving our API all the time! Check the SceneXplain API page for the latest specification.

In the real world, we're not going to spend our time clicking through a web interface to upload images. Rather, we'd use SceneXplain's API. Let's assume we have products.csv in the following format:

"name", "image_url"
"green puffy jacket", "https://images.pexels.com/photos/2529172/pexels-photo-2529172.jpeg"
"long pink coat", "https://images.pexels.com/photos/2043590/pexels-photo-2043590.jpeg"
"white sneakers", "https://images.pexels.com/photos/2946956/pexels-photo-2946956.jpeg"
🗃️
In a real-world CSV you'd have a lot more fields and records. We're just keeping it simple for this example.

The CSV contains links to the following images:

Let’s load up that file and specify our question for each image based on a template QUESTION:

import csv
import http.client
import json
from pprint import print

CSV_FILE = 'products.csv'
QUESTION = 'Write a detailed product description for the {} in this image, focusing on color, style, cut and fit (if mentioned)'

data = []

with open(CSV_FILE) as file:
    products = csv.DictReader(file)

    for product in products:
        product['image'] = product["image_url"]
        product['question'] = QUESTION.format(product['name'])
        product['features'] = ['tts', 'question_answer']
        product['algorithm'] = 'Comet'
        product['languages'] = ['en']
        product['style'] = 'concise'
        data.append(product)

data = {'data': data}

And here’s the code to send that data over to SceneXplain, then convert the result to JSON:

headers = {
    'x-api-key': f'token {YOUR_GENERATED_SECRET}',
    'content-type': 'application/json',
}

connection = http.client.HTTPSConnection('us-central1-causal-diffusion.cloudfunctions.net')
connection.request('POST', '/describe', json.dumps(data), headers)
response = connection.getresponse()

response_data = response.read().decode('utf-8')

connection.close()

data = json.loads(response_data)

Finally, we can write the information we want to a new CSV file:

# extract just the info we want
records = []
for product in data['result']:
    record = {}
    record['name'] = product['name']
    record['image_url'] = product['image_url']
    record['desc_en'] = product['i18n']['en']
    record['audio_en'] = product['tts']['en']
    records.append(record)

# write to CSV
field_names = list(records[0].keys())

with open('products-updated.csv', 'w', newline='') as csvfile:
    writer = csv.DictWriter(csvfile, fieldnames=field_names)

    writer.writeheader()
    writer.writerows(records)

Perfect! Now we have our updated products-updated.csv, containing not just the product name and image URL but also detailed product descriptions and audio transcripts:
