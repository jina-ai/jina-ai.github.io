Rationale 0.6 Update

Discover the future with Rationale 0.6! Our groundbreaking "Multiverse" mode shows the outcomes of your decisions in a causal chain, up to 3 levels deep. See the potential consequences of your choices and make informed decisions. Try Rationale 0.6 today!
Engineering Group • 3 minutes read

Rationale is a decision-making AI powered by the latest version of GPT-3 and in-context learning. It assists business owners, managers, and individuals in making tough decisions by generating a pros and cons list, SWOT analysis, and multi-option analysis. By considering all relevant factors, Rationale helps users make rational decisions.

💡
Please force refresh the browser to see the latest change. This can be usually achieved by Hold ⇧ Shift (MacOS) or Ctrl (Windows/Linux) and click the Reload button.

New outcome analysis mode

Decisions are hard to make because we can't see the outcomes of our decisions. But what if we can? What if we can glimpse the multiverse before you decide? With Rationale 0.6, we bring you the groundbreaking outcomes analysis mode - or we would like to call it the "Multiverse" mode.

With the outcomes analysis, you'll be able to see the outcomes of your decisions in a causal chain, up to 3 levels deep. This tree-like structure will show you not only the immediate outcomes, but also the outcomes of the outcomes, depending on what happens along the way. With this feature, you'll be able to make informed decisions and see the full picture of the impact your choices will have.

Let's see some results in action. Notice how the consequences are unfolded based on what would be happened along the way. The smiley faces in different colors indicate the sentiment of the outcomes.

Credit history

In addition to the Outcomes Analysis mode, we're also introducing Credit History, so you can see exactly when and how you've earned or spent your credits. This new feature will make it even easier to keep track of your credits, so you can be sure to get the most out of your experience with Rationale.
